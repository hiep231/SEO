Why a dedicated workspace matters

When working from home, the boundary between professional and personal life often blurs. A dedicated workspace serves as a psychological trigger: when you sit down at that specific desk, your brain knows it's time to focus. It eliminates the friction of setting up and packing away your laptop every day at the kitchen table.

Furthermore, a dedicated area allows you to optimize ergonomics, control your lighting for video calls, and minimize household distractions. It is an investment in your career longevity and mental well-being. For those with limited space, a compact footprint can be just as effective. Ensure you check out curated tech gear to build the ultimate WFH mini setup.
